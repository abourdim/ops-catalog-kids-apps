## 🎯 Purpose

Train short-term memory and concentration. The game shows a sequence of colored lights with sounds. You must repeat the exact sequence. Each round adds one more step. One mistake and it's over.

This is a pure memory challenge — no math, no reading, no typing. Just watch, listen, and remember. It builds the kind of focused attention that helps in every other subject.

---

## 📖 What You'll Learn

- **Short-term memory** — holding a growing sequence in your head
- **Sequential memory** — remembering order, not just items
- **Audio-visual association** — each color has a unique tone, strengthening memory through two senses
- **Concentration** — one moment of distraction means game over
- **Pattern recognition** — some sequences form patterns you can chunk together
- **Self-improvement** — beating your own high score is deeply motivating

---

## 🎮 How to Play — Step by Step

1. **Press ▶ Play** to start
2. **Watch the board** — one colored quadrant lights up with a tone
3. **Click that same quadrant** to repeat it
4. **Round 2** — the game shows the previous color PLUS one new one
5. **Repeat both** in the exact order
6. **Each round adds one more** to the sequence
7. **One wrong click** = game over, your score is the round you reached
8. **High score** is saved automatically — try to beat it next time

## 🎨 The Board

```
┌──────────┬──────────┐
│          │          │
│   RED    │   BLUE   │
│   (1/Q)  │   (2/W)  │
│          │          │
├──────────┼──────────┤
│          │          │
│  GREEN   │  YELLOW  │
│   (3/A)  │   (4/S)  │
│          │          │
└──────────┴──────────┘
```

Each quadrant has a unique musical tone:
- **Red** — C4 (261 Hz)
- **Blue** — E4 (329 Hz)
- **Green** — G4 (392 Hz)
- **Yellow** — C5 (523 Hz)

## 💡 Tips for Parents & Kids

- **Use BOTH eyes and ears** — the tones help you remember. Close your eyes during playback to focus on the sounds
- **Chunk the sequence** — instead of "red, blue, green, yellow, red" think "red-blue, green-yellow, red"
- **Don't rush your turn** — there's no time limit once it's your turn, take a moment to recall
- **Say the colors in your head** as they light up: "red, blue, blue, green..."
- **Practice daily** — memory improves dramatically with consistent short sessions
- **Milestone: Round 7** is considered good for a child, Round 12+ is excellent
- **Use keyboard shortcuts** (1234 or QWAS) for faster input on longer sequences

## 📊 Scoring

- **Score = round number** — reaching Round 8 means you memorized an 8-step sequence
- **High score persisted** in localStorage — survives browser refresh
- **World-class** Simon performance is 20+ steps

## 🧪 The Science

Simon-type games have been used in cognitive research since the 1970s. They measure "digit span" — the number of items a person can hold in working memory. Average for children:

| Age | Typical Span |
|-----|-------------|
| 4–5 | 3–4 items |
| 6–7 | 4–5 items |
| 8–9 | 5–6 items |
| 10+ | 6–8 items |

Regular practice can increase working memory capacity, which correlates with improved performance in math and reading.
