## 🎯 Purpose

Train visual memory and concentration by finding matching pairs of cards. The board shows face-down cards. Flip two at a time — if they match, they disappear. If not, they flip back and you must remember where they were.

Simple rules, but deep skill. This game trains the exact same working memory used in math, reading, and problem-solving.

---

## 📖 What You'll Learn

- **Visual memory** — remembering the position of images on a grid
- **Concentration** — maintaining focus across many flip attempts
- **Strategic thinking** — choosing which cards to flip based on what you remember
- **Pattern recognition** — in math mode, connecting operations to their results
- **Letter case awareness** — in letter mode, matching uppercase to lowercase
- **Efficiency** — solving in fewer moves means stronger memory

---

## 🎮 How to Play — Step by Step

1. **Choose grid size** from the dropdown (2×2 up to 4×6)
2. **Choose mode**:
   - **🎯 Emoji** — match identical emoji pairs (🚀↔🚀)
   - **🔤 Letters** — match uppercase to lowercase (A↔a)
   - **🔢 Math** — match operations to results (3×2↔6)
3. **Press ▶ New Game**
4. **Click any card** to flip it — see what's underneath
5. **Click a second card** to flip it
6. **Match!** → both cards disappear with a success sound ✓
7. **No match** → both cards flip back after a brief pause ✗
8. **Remember** where each card was — you'll need that information later
9. **Clear all pairs** to win
10. **Your score** = number of moves (fewer is better)

## 🎮 Game Modes

### 🎯 Emoji Mode (easiest)
Match identical pairs: 🚀↔🚀, ⚡↔⚡, 🦁↔🦁

Best for: ages 3+, beginners, warming up

### 🔤 Letter Mode
Match uppercase to lowercase: A↔a, B↔b, K↔k

Best for: ages 4+, learning the alphabet, letter recognition

### 🔢 Math Mode (hardest)
Match operations to their results:
- 1+1 ↔ 2
- 3×2 ↔ 6
- 9-3 ↔ 6
- 8÷2 ↔ 4

Best for: ages 6+, combining memory with arithmetic

## 💡 Tips for Parents & Kids

- **Start with 2×2 emoji** for the youngest — success builds confidence
- **Use a strategy**: scan the whole board first, then flip systematically (row by row)
- **When you flip a non-match**, don't be frustrated — you just learned where two cards are
- **Math mode is sneaky** — you must calculate AND remember positions simultaneously
- **Track your move count** — try to beat your personal best on each grid size
- **Perfect game on 4×4** = 8 moves (every flip is a match). Realistic goal: under 16 moves
- **Play before homework** — 5 minutes of memory warms up the brain

## 📊 Scoring

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Moves** | Total card flips (2 cards = 1 move). Fewer = better memory |
| **Pairs found** | Progress tracker (X out of Y) |
| **Perfect game** | Moves = number of pairs (every flip is a match) |

| Grid | Good | Great | Perfect |

| 4×6 (12 pairs) | 28 moves | 18 moves | 12 moves |
